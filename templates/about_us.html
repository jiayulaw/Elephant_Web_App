About Us

System Overview


Motivation

Human wild-life conflict (HWC) is a worldwide issue. Asian elephants (Elephas maximus) are categorised as endangered species by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) Red List of Threatened Species in 2014. With a large part of Malaysia rainforests converted for agriculture purpose over the past decades, elephant habitats shrinked significantly, increasing the occurrence of human-elephant conflict (HEC). Crop raiding by elephants led to million-ringgits economic losses yearly. Current methods of elephant deterrent such as the use of trenches and electrical fences are ineffective, environmental intrusive, and costly.

Introduction

The Elephant Intrusion Detection and Deterrent System (EIDDS) project aims to leverage on Wireless Sensor Network (WSN) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) technology to mitigate HEC. The proposed WSN is composed of end devices and a gateway. The AI enabled end device is equipped with a set of sensing, actuating, and communication units, enabling real-time elephant detection & deterrent as well as user alert functionality during both day and night time. The end device is housed in a 3D printed housing and powered by both solar and lead acid batteries to support the 24/7 system operation. EIDDS also provides users a dedicated web application for convenient system monitoring over the internet.

Design Features & Considerations

  • Cost efficient
  • 24/7 operation
  • Computer vision with active learning capability
  • Real time elephant deterrent
  • WSN with image transmission capability
  • End users notifications
  • Web application for data access and device monitoring
  • Off-grid power supply system
  • 3D printed weather proof housing

Future Development

  • Acoustic-based elephant detection.
  • Tree topology WSN for greater coverage area.
  • Lower power edge computing device (Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W).
  • Li-ion batteries to improve energy density and reduce overall physical sizing of housing.
  • Custom PCB for efficient arrangement and reliable performance.
  • Use of readily available watertight project boxes to reduce cost.
